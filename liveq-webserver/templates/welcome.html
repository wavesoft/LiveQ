What is this?

The Virtual Atom Smasher is an e-learning game, developed by CERN in collaboration with other partners, members of the Citizen Cyberlab. Using this game, citizens can not only learn about particle physics and the internals of CERN, but also contribute back to the fundamental research.

You don't need to have any scientific background. Everybody can help and learn in the process!

Learn more

How can I help?

The theoretical physicists at CERN are creating mathematical models for describing how nature works. However even after they are built, they sill have a lot of parameters that needs to be tuned in order for them to accurately reflect reality. We are therefore inviting you to help the scientists with their task. You will be given the real scientific tools used at CERN and you will have to tune the simulations until the results match the experimental data collected from the big experiments!

Learn more

The technology behind?

This prototype is using various technologies developed by CERN and other partners. The high-energy particle collisions are simulated by the Pythia generator and they are analyzed with the Rivet toolkit. These software run on computing nodes in the CERN data center or on volunteers around the world. These software are managed by the LiveQ framework which takes away the complex operations.
